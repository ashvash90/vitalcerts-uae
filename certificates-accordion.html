Standard Translations

Used mainly for reference purposes (to understand the content of the document). For example, if you had a document in a language you aren’t familiar with and you just need to understand what it says, a normal translation would be the way to go.

Legal Translations

A type of translation that will be legally accepted by any government authority. There are only legal translators available for certain languages, however. When the document is required to be submitted to the Ministry of Justice (MOJ) or the Courts, legal translation is mandatory.

Certified Translations

Similar to a Legal Translation but cannot be submitted to the Ministry of Justice (MOJ) or the Courts. Certified translations are useful for certain scenarios when an official legal translator is not available in the UAE for a particular language (e.g. Portuguese, Hindi, Czech).

How do I choose between a legal and certified translation?

If you're required to submit a document to the Ministry of Justice (MOJ) or the Courts, you'll need a Legal Translation. A Certified Translation cannot be submitted to the Ministry of Justice or Courts, while a Legal Translation can. Keep in mind that Legal Translations are only available in certain languages though.
